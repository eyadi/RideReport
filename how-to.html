How to create your
Ride Report

Turn any cycling activity into a stunning PNG — map, power chart, and stats — ready to post on Strava or share with your club.

Best experience in Google Chrome
Step 1
Load your ride

There are two ways to get your ride into the app — pick whichever suits you.

Via Strava

Connect your Strava

Click Connect with Strava on the landing screen, log in, and your three most recent rides appear instantly. Click one to load it.

One-time setup. Your login is remembered.

Via File

Drop a file

Drag a .fit file from your Garmin, Wahoo, or Suunto onto the drop zone — or click Browse File to pick one.

Also accepts Strava JSON files downloaded from the companion page.

Step 2
Customise your report

Your report is fully interactive. Pan and zoom the map, then personalise it before exporting.

Map & theme

Use the Map menu to switch between dark, light, maritime, and navy map styles. Pan and zoom freely.

Background photo

Open Map → My Photo to replace the map with one of your own photos, or blend a photo behind the map.

Custom title

Type a title in the Custom Title box on the landing screen before loading your ride. It appears in the title bubble.

Designer mode

Enable Designer → Designer Mode to drag the stat panels, title, and chart anywhere on the card.

Power chart & FTP

Set your FTP (threshold watts) on the landing screen. The chart colours each second by power zone automatically.

Save your layout

Once you're happy, go to Designer → Save Layout. Load it next time to restore your exact panel positions and settings.

Step 3
Export your PNG

Click Export Screen

The toolbar at the top of your report has an ⬇ Export Screen button. Click it and your browser will download a PNG at the full resolution of your screen — ready to post on Strava, Instagram, or your club WhatsApp.

1

Pan & zoom the map to your preferred view

The exported image captures exactly what you see. Tilt the map with the pitch slider in the Designer menu for a dramatic 3D look.

2

Click ⬇ Export Screen

A short processing spinner appears, then your browser automatically downloads ridename_v6.28.png.

3

Share it

Post directly to Strava as an activity photo, drop it in your club WhatsApp, or share on Instagram. The 16:9 format is perfect for all platforms.

Tips
Good to know
No data is stored. Everything happens in your browser. Close the tab and it's gone — nothing is saved to any server.
Chrome gives the best results. Safari on Mac has a known rendering limitation. Chrome on any platform works perfectly.
No power meter? The power chart and watt stats simply stay blank. Distance, time, elevation, and HR still show normally.
FTP matters for colours. Set your actual FTP on the landing screen so the chart zones (recovery → anaerobic) are calibrated to you, not defaults.
Weather button. Click ☁ Weather in the toolbar to automatically fetch the conditions for the time and location of your ride.
Layouts are reusable. Save a layout once, load it every time — your panels, map position, and theme are all restored instantly.
